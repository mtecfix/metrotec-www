Why Software Patches Are Critical for Business Security

How regular patching prevents 85% of targeted cyberattacks on Metro Detroit businesses

By MetroTec Team • March 01, 2026 • 7 min read

Software patches are the seatbelt of cybersecurity—simple, often overlooked, but absolutely critical when you need them. Metro Detroit businesses face constant threats from unpatched vulnerabilities, with 85% of targeted attacks exploiting known security flaws that patches would have prevented.

The challenge isn't awareness—most IT teams know patching matters. The challenge is execution. Businesses struggle with patch testing, deployment windows, and the fear of breaking critical systems. Yet the cost of not patching far exceeds the inconvenience of maintenance windows.

Why Patches Matter More Than Ever

Attackers scan the internet for vulnerable systems within hours of patch releases. They know many businesses delay updates, creating a window of opportunity. Ransomware groups specifically target unpatched systems, knowing these organizations likely have other security gaps.

Modern patches address more than bugs—they fix security vulnerabilities, improve performance, and add features. Skipping patches means missing critical protections against evolving threats. A Troy manufacturer learned this lesson after ransomware exploited a three-month-old unpatched vulnerability, costing $180,000 in downtime and recovery.

Common Patching Challenges

Testing concerns top the list. Businesses worry patches will break applications or cause compatibility issues. This fear leads to delayed patching, ironically creating bigger problems. The solution is structured testing—patch non-critical systems first, monitor for issues, then roll out broadly.

Maintenance windows create scheduling headaches. Business hours can't accommodate downtime, but after-hours patching requires overtime. Cloud-based systems and virtualization enable rolling updates with minimal disruption. Critical systems can failover to redundant servers during patching.

Patch tracking becomes overwhelming with dozens of systems and hundreds of applications. Manual tracking fails—automated patch management tools identify missing patches, test deployments, and report compliance. These systems pay for themselves by preventing a single security incident.

Effective Patch Management Strategy

Prioritize critical security patches. Not all patches are equal—security updates addressing actively exploited vulnerabilities need immediate deployment. Microsoft's Patch Tuesday releases include severity ratings. Focus on critical and high-severity patches first.

Automate where possible. Operating system patches can deploy automatically with proper testing. Application patches may need more oversight, but automation handles identification and scheduling. Automation ensures consistency and reduces human error.

Establish clear policies. Define maximum time from patch release to deployment—typically 30 days for normal patches, 7 days for critical security updates. Document exceptions and require approval for delayed patching. Policies create accountability.

Test systematically. Maintain a test environment mirroring production systems. Deploy patches to test systems first, run automated tests, and monitor for 24-48 hours before production deployment. This catches issues before they impact business operations.

Measuring Patch Management Success

Track time-to-patch metrics. Measure days from patch release to deployment. Industry best practice is 30 days for normal patches, 7 days for critical. Southfield businesses achieving these targets report 70% fewer security incidents than those with slower patching.

Monitor patch coverage percentage. What percentage of systems have current patches? Aim for 95%+ coverage. The remaining 5% should be documented exceptions with compensating controls. Regular reporting keeps patching visible to management.

Effective patch management isn't glamorous, but it's fundamental. Like wearing a seatbelt, the value becomes obvious when you need it. Metro Detroit businesses with disciplined patching programs avoid the majority of preventable security incidents.
