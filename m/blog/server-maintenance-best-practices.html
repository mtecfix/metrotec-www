Server Maintenance: Preventing Costly Downtime

Scheduled maintenance costs 10x less than emergency repairs

By MetroTec Team

Why Server Maintenance Can't Be Skipped

Servers are the backbone of your business. They store your data, run your applications, and keep your team connected. Yet many Metro Detroit businesses treat server maintenance as optional — until something breaks. Reactive maintenance costs 10x more than scheduled maintenance, and unplanned downtime averages $5,600 per minute for small businesses.

Monthly Maintenance Checklist

Effective server maintenance includes: applying security patches within 48 hours of release, reviewing event logs for warnings and errors, checking disk space and performance metrics, testing backup integrity, updating antivirus definitions, reviewing user access permissions, and documenting any configuration changes. These tasks take 2-3 hours monthly but prevent days of downtime.

Hardware Health Monitoring

Physical servers need attention too. Check drive health using S.M.A.R.T. diagnostics, monitor CPU and memory temperatures, inspect cooling fans and clean dust filters quarterly, and verify UPS battery health. A failed drive with no warning is rare — most drives show warning signs weeks before failure if you're monitoring.

When to Upgrade vs. Maintain

Servers older than 5 years cost more to maintain than to replace. Parts become scarce, performance degrades, and modern software may not run efficiently. MetroTec helps Metro Detroit businesses plan server lifecycles so upgrades happen on your schedule, not in response to a crisis.
